Resource

Office of Community Standards and Integrity 412-268-2140

How They Can Help

  • Discuss ways to respond to incidents where academic dishonesty may have occurred.

  • Advise you on the appeal process.

  • Answer your questions about academic integrity policy

  • Help you to understand consequences of violations.

Office of Community Standards and Integrity 412-268-2140

  • Provide strategies you can use to deal more effectively with course work before it affects your academic success.

  • Talk with you about personal circumstances that may affect course work.

Academic Development 412-268-6878

  • Conduct workshops and tutoring on study skills, time management, and reading skills.

  • Provide Fast Facts on study skills.

  • Identify academic difficulties which may be due to learning disabilities.

  • Act as an advocate for students with learning disabilities.

Intercultural Communications Center 412-268-4979

  • Counsel students having trouble with academic or personal stress.

  • Help you to find peer tutoring.

  • Arrange for programs on time management, study skills or other academic success programs for any group of students (e.g. a residence hall floor, a student organization, or simply a group of friends).

University Libraries 412-268-2442

  • Advise students on proper research methodology.
